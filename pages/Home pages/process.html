Process & Hiring

 
The process and hiring in an IT (Information Technology) interview typically follows a structured format, 
            especially for fresher and campus placements. Here's a breakdown of the typical IT interview process and what 
            you can expect:
  1. Application or Campus Placement Drive

    • Campus Placement: Companies visit colleges and shortlist candidates based on eligibility (marks, branch, no backlogs).

    • Off-Campus: You apply through job portals like Naukri, LinkedIn, company websites, etc.
  2. Online Aptitude Test / Coding Round

    • Aptitude Test: Logical reasoning, quantitative aptitude, verbal ability.

    • Coding Test: Based on languages like C, C++, Java, or Python. You may get 2â€“3 coding problems on platforms like HackerRank, Codility, etc.
  3. Technical Interview

    • Focus: Your core knowledge, problem-solving skills, and projects.

    • Common Topics:
    1. Data Structures & Algorithms (DSA)

    2. DBMS (SQL queries, normalization)

    3. Operating Systems

    4. Computer Network

    5. OOP concepts

    6. Programming Languages (C, C++, Python, Java)

    7. Projects (Be ready to explain your project in detail)
  4. HR Interview

    • Focus: Your personality, goals, communication skills, and culture fit.

    • Common HR Topics:
    1. Tell me about yourself.

    2. Why should we hire you?

    3. Where do you see yourself in 5 years?

    4. Are you comfortable relocating?

    5. Expected salary (for off-campus or experienced roles)
  5. Managerial / Final Round (Sometimes)

    • Focus on how you handle pressure, team work, real-life problem solving.

    • Situational and behavioral questions.
