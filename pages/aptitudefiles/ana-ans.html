Answers & Explanations – Analogy Page

Ans1. Answer: Tiger
Explanation: A dog barks; a tiger roars. These are the characteristic sounds each animal makes.

Ans2. Answer: Cut
Explanation: A pen is used to write, just as a knife is used to cut. These are the primary functions of each tool.

Ans3. Answer: Galaxy
Explanation: A tree is a part of a forest; a star is part of a galaxy. Both show part-to-whole relationships.

Ans4. Answer: Hunger
Explanation: Water quenches thirst; food satisfies hunger. Both are cause-effect relationships.

Ans5. Answer: Cap
Explanation: A shoe covers the foot; a cap covers the head. Both are items worn on a body part.

Ans6. Answer: Hear
Explanation: Eyes are used to see; ears are used to hear. This is a function analogy.

Ans7. Answer: Teacher
Explanation: A doctor works in a hospital; a teacher works in a school. Both are profession–place relationships.

Ans8. Answer: Ink
Explanation: A needle needs thread to work; a pen needs ink to write. These are tool–medium relationships.

Ans9. Answer: Temperature
Explanation: A clock measures time; a thermometer measures temperature. Both are instrument–measurement relationships.

Ans10. Answer: Articles
Explanation: A book consists of pages; a newspaper consists of articles. Both are content–container analogies.
